Developmental Milestones

Infants (0-12 months)

  • Babies learn to grasp objects and babble.
  • They start responding to their name and sitting without support.
  • By around 12 months, they may begin saying their first words and taking their first steps.

Parenting Tips

Tip 1: Bonding with Your Baby

Take time to cuddle, talk, and sing to your baby every day. This helps them feel safe and loved.

Tip 2: Creating a Routine

Establishing a predictable schedule for feeding, napping, and bedtime helps your baby feel secure and comfortable.

Tip 3: Supporting Development

Provide age-appropriate toys and activities that stimulate your baby's senses and encourage exploration.

FAQ

What are developmental milestones?

Developmental milestones are behavioral or physical checkpoints in children's development as they grow. They help track children's progress in areas like movement, speech, play, and social skills.

How can I support my child's development?

You can support your child's development by providing a safe and loving environment, engaging in interactive play, reading together, and encouraging healthy habits like good nutrition and sleep.

When should I be concerned about my child's development?

If you notice your child is not meeting developmental milestones for their age, talk to your pediatrician. Early intervention can be crucial in addressing any potential developmental issues.
